History of Weather Forecasting

1. When and Why Weather Forecasting Was Created

Weather forecasting began in the early 1800s when scientists needed a way to predict storms, protect ships, and reduce deaths from severe weather. Early forecasts were based on basic observations of wind direction, clouds, and air pressure.

2. How Weather Forecasting Has Grown Over the Years

  • Telegraph technology in the 1840s allowed weather data to be shared quickly for the first time.
  • In 1870, the U.S. Weather Bureau was created.
  • Radar was introduced in the 1940s to track storms.
  • Satellites in the 1960s made global forecasting possible.
  • Computer models from the 1970s–2000s greatly increased prediction accuracy.
  • Modern APIs now deliver real-time, location-based weather data instantly.

3. Where Weather Forecasting Stands Today

Today, weather forecasting relies on satellites, radar, ocean buoys, and advanced computer models. Real-time data is available through APIs like OpenWeather, allowing websites and apps (like SkyCast) to provide instant forecasts anywhere in the world.
