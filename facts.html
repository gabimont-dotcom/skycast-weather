Cool Weather Facts

  • The highest temperature ever recorded on Earth was 134°F (56.7°C) in Death Valley.
  • The coldest recorded temperature was −128.6°F (−89.2°C) in Antarctica.
  • A single lightning bolt is five times hotter than the sun’s surface.
  • Hurricanes rotate counterclockwise in the Northern Hemisphere.
  • The U.S. experiences more tornadoes than any other country.
  • Fog is actually a low-lying cloud sitting on the ground.
  • The fastest wind speed ever recorded was 253 mph during Cyclone Olivia.
  • Raindrops can fall as fast as 22 mph.
  • Weather forecasting accuracy has doubled in the past 20 years.
  • Clouds can weigh over a million pounds.
  • The jet stream moves storms across the U.S. at around 100–200 mph.
  • Thunder is caused by the rapid expansion of air around lightning.
